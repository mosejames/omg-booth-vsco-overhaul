Section 01

The Opportunity

What changes when AI writes your client emails instead of templates.

Right now, every lead gets the same template email with their name swapped in. A corporate client planning a 500-person brand activation gets the same language as someone booking a birthday party photo booth. The VSCO token system ({{primary_contact.first_name}}) handles mail-merge personalization, but it doesn't understand context.

AI reads the actual form answers (event type, guest count, vibe, what they care about) and writes a response that references their specific event. Same brand voice every time, but every email feels like you sat down and wrote it yourself.

Without AI: Template

"Hey Jordan, thanks for reaching out! We'd love to learn more about your event. Here's a quick questionnaire to help us build your custom proposal."

Same copy for every single lead.

With AI: Personalized

"Hey Jordan, a 250-person corporate gala with a black-tie dress code and gold/black color scheme? That's right in our lane. We've done similar setups for companies like yours. Elegant booth design, branded overlays, and instant social sharing so your guests are posting before dessert. Let's nail down the details."

References their event type, guest count, vibe, and features.

Form Changes

The AI is only as good as the data it gets. These additions give it the context to write emails that feel personal, not generated.

Every field below serves double duty: it helps you build better proposals AND gives AI the context to write better emails. Nothing here is added "just for AI."

Form 1: Lead Intake | Add 2 Fields

Keep the form under 90 seconds. These two additions are high-signal, low-friction.

Add This FieldTypeOptionsWhat AI Does With It
How did you hear about us? Dropdown + "Other" text Referral, Saw us at an event, Instagram, Google, Event planner, Other "Since you saw our setup at the Delta event..." or "Your planner has great taste sending you our way." Instant rapport in the first line.
What should guests walk away remembering? Open text This is the emotional hook. "I want everyone laughing and posting" gives AI completely different material than "sophisticated brand impression." Replaces the generic "Tell us about your event."
Why replace "Tell us about your event": That question gets logistical answers like "it's a company party on April 15th." You already collect date and type via other fields. The new question gets emotional, aspirational answers that AI turns into compelling copy.

Section 03

How It Works

The technical architecture. VSCO Workspace's built-in "Post Web Request" automation action sends data directly to Make.com. No Zapier subscription needed.

VSCO Workspace
Lead status changes to "Qualified"
You review the lead and mark them as qualified. This triggers a VSCO automation with a Post Web Request action, a built-in webhook that sends the lead data (name, event type, guest count, form answers) to an external URL.
Make.com (Free Tier)
Receives the webhook, prepares the AI prompt
Make.com catches the incoming data and structures it into a prompt for Claude. The prompt includes your brand voice instructions, package details, and the client's specific form answers. This is a visual workflow. Drag-and-drop, no code.
Claude API
Generates personalized email + SMS drafts
Claude reads the client's event details and writes two things: a personalized email (subject + body) and a matching SMS message (under 155 characters). Both in your brand voice, both referencing the same event details. Cost: ~$0.03-0.10 per lead.
Your Phone
You review both drafts via email notification
Make.com sends you an email with the AI-generated email draft AND SMS draft side-by-side, plus two links: "Approve & Send" and "Edit First." One tap approves both. Full details in the Review Workflow section.
Send
Client receives personalized email + SMS
Make.com sends the approved email via Gmail and the SMS via Quo's API. If the client opted for "Text" or "Both" contact preference, they get both channels. Email-only clients just get the email. The client gets a response that references their specific event within minutes of your approval.

Section 04

Review Workflow

How you review AI drafts from your phone without logging into anything. One email notification, one tap.

You don't want to open a dashboard, log into a tool, or navigate a spreadsheet every time a lead comes in. The review process should be as fast as checking a text message.

How It Works

When the AI generates drafts, Make.com sends you an email. The email contains:

What's in the Notification Email

  • Client name and event type at the top, so you know who it's for instantly
  • Key details like date, guest count, budget range (so you can sanity-check without opening VSCO)
  • The full AI-generated email draft formatted exactly as the client would receive it
  • The AI-generated SMS draft shown below the email, so you can review both at once
  • Contact preference showing if client selected Email, Text, or Both
  • "Approve & Send" button that links to a Make.com webhook and sends both email and SMS (based on client preference)
  • "Edit First" button that opens a Google Sheet row where you can modify either draft, then approve

The Three Paths

Path 1: Approve (90% of the time)

Both drafts look good. Tap "Approve & Send Both." Make.com receives the webhook, sends the email via Gmail, and sends the SMS via Quo (if client chose Text or Both). Done. Total time: 15 seconds.

Path 2: Quick Edit (9% of the time)

One draft needs a tweak. Tap "Edit First" and it opens a Google Sheet on your phone with both the email and SMS text in editable cells. Fix either one, tap a checkbox to approve. Make.com watches the sheet and sends the edited versions. Total time: 1-2 minutes.

Path 3: Skip (1% of the time)

Something's off. Wrong tone, bad data, whatever. Ignore the email. Nothing gets sent. Go into VSCO and handle it manually. The system only sends when you explicitly approve.

Nothing sends without your approval. AI generates drafts, not live emails. You are always the last step. This is not auto-pilot. It's a co-pilot that does the writing and waits for you to say "send."

The Google Sheet: Your Drafts Dashboard

Every AI draft gets logged to a Google Sheet automatically. This serves three purposes:

  • Review queue: See all pending drafts in one place. Each row has: client name, event type, email draft, SMS draft, contact preference, status (Pending / Approved / Edited / Skipped), timestamp.
  • Edit surface: When you tap "Edit First," it opens straight to the right row. Email draft and SMS draft are in separate editable cells. Fix either one, check the "Approved" box, done.
  • History log: Every AI-generated message is recorded. See what was sent (email, SMS, or both), when, and whether you edited it. Over time, this tells you how good the AI drafts are.
Why not SMS for review? Your instinct was right. Phone-based review is the way. But SMS has character limits (160 chars), can't show formatted email previews, and requires Twilio ($). Email notification gives you both the email AND SMS draft preview, works on your phone, and costs nothing. Same speed, better experience.
Section 05

What AI Writes

The system prompt that controls Claude's voice, the email and SMS drafts it generates, and example outputs for different client types.

The System Prompt (Your Brand Voice)

This lives in Make.com as a text field. It tells Claude who OMG Booth is and how to write. You edit this to dial in the tone.

System Prompt

You are writing emails on behalf of OMG Booth, an experiential photo and media activation company based in Atlanta with 14+ years in the game. We're not just dropping off a booth. We're designing an experience that captures attention, collects data, and keeps your brand in the conversation long after the event ends. Voice guidelines: - Direct, confident, warm but not overly casual. Think strategic partner, not vendor. - Use "we" not "I." You're representing the team. - Reference the client's specific event details naturally. Don't force it, but make it clear you read what they wrote. - Keep emails concise: 3-5 short paragraphs max. - Lead with what makes their event exciting, not with your company bio. - End with a clear next step (fill out the form, review the proposal, etc.). - Never use exclamation marks more than once per email. - Never say "I'm an AI" or "as an AI." You are OMG Booth. - Never use em dashes in any output. Use periods, commas, or restructured sentences instead. - Never use corporate fluff (synergy, circle back, low-hanging fruit) or hype phrases (game changer, crushing it, disrupting). - I don't oversell. I position. Present value clearly and let the work speak for itself. Pricing rules: - The pricing floor is $1,200. Nothing goes out the door below $1,200. - Never hedge on pricing. Never offer discounts. Never say "let me know if budget is a concern." - If a lead's budget is below $1,200, acknowledge the gap honestly and position upward. Don't chase budget leads downhill. - Budget tiers for reference: $1,200-$2,500, $2,500-$4,000, $4,000-$7,000, $7,000-$15,000, $15,000+. Package knowledge: - All packages include 3 hours. Additional hours are $350/hr. - Essential Experience: $1,800. 3hr booth, photos+GIFs, overlay, digital delivery, 1 attendant. - Premium Experience: $2,800. 3hr, photos+GIFs+video, backdrop, prints, social wall, 2 attendants. - Full Activation: $4,200. 3hr, 360+standard booth, slo-mo, full branding, lead capture, 3 staff. - The price reflects the experience scope, not the time. Every tier starts at 3 hours. When recommending a package, match to their guest count, budget, and priorities. Recommend what fits. Don't push the most expensive option just because you can. Expo/Trade Show context: - For expo or trade show leads, emphasize data capture, lead generation, and brand visibility. - These are typically higher-value engagements ($5,000-$15,000+). - Position OMG Booth as a strategic activation partner, not a novelty rental. - Reference ROI, foot traffic, and post-event data collection when relevant. Multi-City Tour context: - For tour or multi-market leads, reference scalability, consistency across markets, and dedicated account management. - These engagements often involve repeat activations and longer-term partnerships. - Emphasize logistics expertise and turnkey execution across cities. SMS guidelines: - Also generate a matching SMS message for each touchpoint. - SMS must be under 155 characters (1 segment). This is a hard limit. - SMS tone: slightly more casual than email. Warm, punchy, personal. - Reference one specific detail from their event (type, vibe, or name). - End with a clear nudge (check inbox, check your portal, etc.). - Never include links in SMS. The email has the links. - Never use em dashes in SMS. Use periods or commas instead. - Return SMS in a separate "sms_draft" field.

Section 06

Setup Guide

Step-by-step. Complete the overhaul plan's form changes first, then layer in AI.

Prerequisite: The AI layer sits on top of the overhaul plan. It doesn't replace it. Complete the core system first (Forms 1-3, Upgradable Quote, "Qualified" lead status, automations). Then add AI as an enhancement. Think of it as version 2.0.

Add the New Form Fields

Update Form 1 and Form 2 in VSCO Workspace with the 6 new fields from Section 02. These fields feed the AI but also improve your manual workflow.

  • Form 1: Add "How did you hear about us?" (dropdown) and replace "Tell us about your event" with "What should guests walk away remembering?" (open text)
  • Form 2: Add "Who are your guests?" (dropdown), "What matters most?" (multi-select), "What's the vibe?" (dropdown + text), "Any inspiration?" (optional text)
  • Create matching custom fields in Settings › Custom Fields for any new fields that should map to tokens

Create a Make.com Account

Sign up at make.com (free tier). Create a new scenario called "OMG Booth AI Emails."

  • Make.com free tier: 1,000 operations/month, 2 active scenarios
  • At ~30 leads/month, you'll use ~180 operations. Well within limits.
  • The scenario will have 5 modules: Webhook → Claude API → Google Sheet → Email notification → (wait for approval) → Send

Get a Claude API Key

Sign up at console.anthropic.com. Create an API key. Add a small prepaid credit ($10 will last months).

  • Use Claude Haiku for fastest/cheapest: ~$0.01-0.03 per lead
  • Use Claude Sonnet for better quality: ~$0.03-0.10 per lead
  • $10 prepaid credit = ~100-300 leads depending on model. At 30 leads/month, that's 3-10 months.

Build the Make.com Scenario

The visual workflow. Each box is a "module" in Make.com's interface.

Module 1: Webhook (Trigger)

Create a custom webhook in Make.com. Copy the webhook URL. This is the URL you'll paste into VSCO Workspace.

Module 2: HTTP Request to Claude API

  • URL: https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages
  • Method: POST
  • Headers: x-api-key: [your key], anthropic-version: 2023-06-01, content-type: application/json
  • Body: Your system prompt (brand voice + SMS instructions) + the lead data from Module 1 as the user message
  • Model: claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 (fast/cheap) or claude-sonnet-4-6 (best quality)
  • Response format: Claude returns JSON with email_subject, email_body, and sms_draft fields

Module 3: Google Sheets | Add Row

Log both drafts to a Google Sheet: client name, event type, date, contact preference, AI email subject, AI email body, AI SMS draft, status = "Pending", timestamp, approve webhook URL.

Module 4: Gmail | Send Notification

Send yourself an email with the client details, full AI email draft, AI SMS draft, contact preference badge, and two buttons: Approve (sends both) and Edit (link to the Google Sheet row).

Build the Approval Scenario

A second Make.com scenario that handles what happens when you tap "Approve."

  • Module 1: Webhook (triggered by your Approve button click)
  • Module 2: Google Sheets, read the draft row, get the email and SMS text
  • Module 3: Gmail, send the approved email to the client
  • Module 4: Router, check contact preference field
  • Module 4a: If "Text" or "Both" → HTTP Request to Quo API to send the SMS
  • Module 5: Google Sheets, update status to "Sent"

This uses your second free scenario slot on Make.com's free tier. The Quo SMS send is a simple HTTP POST to their API.

Wire Up the VSCO Automation

Create the automation in VSCO Workspace that triggers everything.

  • Observable: Lead › Lead Status Changed
  • Execute: Immediately
  • Condition: Lead status = "Qualified"
  • Action: Post Web Request → paste your Make.com webhook URL

The web request sends lead and contact data to Make.com. Make.com handles everything from there.

Test with a Dummy Lead

Create a test lead in VSCO, fill out Forms 1 and 2 with realistic data, change status to "Qualified," and watch the pipeline:

  • Did Make.com receive the webhook? (Check execution history)
  • Did Claude generate a good draft? (Check the Google Sheet)
  • Did you receive the notification email?
  • Does "Approve" send the email to the test contact?
  • Is the status updated to "Sent" in the sheet?

Iterate on the system prompt until the drafts consistently sound right. Most of the tuning happens here.
