# VSCO Workspace - Glossary of Terms

**Actions**: What an automation performs. Actions include adding an event, adding a task, sending an email, updating a field, or posting a web request.

**Bookable Event**: Part of the Scheduling feature; a scheduler you set up with certain availability for clients to book a meeting, call, session, or mini session.

**Booking Site**: A public page listing your available bookable events.

**Brand**: A distinct identity within your account with its own branding, email signature, and client-facing look. One account can have multiple brands.

**Client**: The individual hiring you for the job; the recipient of invoices, contracts, etc.

**Client Access**: Any page where clients interact with Workspace: contact forms, questionnaires, contracts, quotes, invoices, payment pages, and the client portal.

**Client Portal**: A single page where clients can view their open leads, jobs, financial items (invoices), and upcoming interactions.

**Closed**: If a lead does not book or a job cancels, it should be closed (not completed). Closing voids outstanding invoices.

**Completed**: The final stage of a successfully fulfilled job. Different from Closed.

**Conditions**: Rules that narrow down what is required for an automation to run.

**Contact Relationship**: How you connect contacts in your Address Book. Related contacts appear in dropdowns for quotes, emails, event attendees, etc.

**Custom Fields**: User-created fields for Job Profiles or Contact Profiles, extending the default data model.

**Deliverable Workflow**: A list of tasks tied to a specific product, which only appear on a job when that product is ordered.

**Event Channel**: How an event takes place -- In Person, Over the Phone, or Virtually.

**Event Type**: The type of events (sessions, meetings, other interactions) on your job schedules and calendar feeds. Three base categories: Call, Meeting, Session.

**Execution History**: Shows if an automation failed or triggered successfully, with reasons why an execution was skipped.

**Invoice**: A document requesting specific payment from the client on an order. You can have more than one invoice based on a payment schedule.

**Job**: A lead that has been booked (client agreed to work with you).

**Job Role**: A role that you, your team, or clients occupy for a job (e.g., Primary Contact, Venue Contact, DJ).

**Job Stage**: The four stages a job progresses through: Lead, Booked, Fulfillment, Completed.

**Job Type**: Categories defining different types of services you offer (e.g., weddings, headshots, commercial).

**Lead**: Anyone who makes contact with your studio but has not agreed to do business yet. Leads come in through contact forms or are entered manually.

**Lead Source**: Tracks where your leads are coming from (e.g., Google, referral, Instagram).

**Lead Status**: Used to track where the lead is during the sales process; statuses can be customized with rules.

**Mini Session**: A bookable event with specific dates and time slots (unlike regular bookable events which use availability schedules).

**Observing (Trigger)**: The event or occurrence an automation looks for to trigger it to run.

**Order**: An overview of the entire set of products or services ordered by the client. Once a quote is approved, it turns into an order.

**Package**: A pre-configured bundle of products/services that can be added to quotes.

**Payment Schedule**: Determines how an order is broken into invoices (number and due dates). E.g., 100% now = one invoice; 50% retainer + balance later = two invoices.

**Profit Center**: A category of work at your studio for reporting purposes (can be broad or specific).

**Purge**: Permanently delete a closed lead/job from the database.

**Questionnaire**: A form for collecting information from clients. Can be public or private, embedded on websites.

**Task**: An individual action item within a task list.

**Task List**: A grouping/subcategory of tasks. All tasks live under task lists.

**Tokens**: Placeholders in double curly brackets `{{ }}` that link to specific fields of data on a job or contact profile. Used in emails, questionnaires, contracts, and automations.

**Workflow**: The set of tasks to complete throughout the process of taking a client from new lead to completed job.
