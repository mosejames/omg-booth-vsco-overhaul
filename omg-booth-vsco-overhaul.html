How It Works

Every new lead enters through Form 1 (public, embedded on your website). After you qualify them and change their lead status, Form 2 fires automatically to collect event specifics needed for the proposal.

You use their Form 2 answers to build an Upsell Quote — a single link where the client sees product cards with images, selects their package, adds optional upgrades (360°, slo-mo, print station), fills out Form 3 (logistics), signs the contract, and pays — all in one flow. No back-and-forth.

As the event approaches, Form 4 collects design assets and branding details 2-3 weeks out. Your team uses Form 5 (pre-event) and Form 6 (post-event) internally for operational reporting, triggered automatically around the event date.

No conditional logic: VSCO Workspace doesn't support show/hide fields based on previous answers. The new forms use clear Headline and Paragraph layout fields to create labeled sections with instructions like "Skip to the next section if not applicable." This is clean, simple, and works.

Communication Channels

Email is the document layer — forms, contracts, invoices, detailed information. SMS is the notification layer — fast nudges, milestone alerts, and quick check-ins. Clients choose their preference on Form 1. Both channels run in parallel via Quo integration.

Section 03

Product & Quote Setup

Build the products, packages, and add-ons that power the upsell quote experience. When products have images, VSCO Workspace renders them as visual cards clients can browse and select.

Why this matters: VSCO Workspace's "Upgradable" quote type displays products as cards with images, descriptions, and prices. The client picks a package, adds upgrades, fills out the embedded logistics questionnaire, signs the contract, and pays — all in one link. This replaces sending separate quotes, questionnaires, and contracts as individual emails.
Audit finding: 80+ products exist in VSCO Workspace across categories (Activation Fees, Add-Ons, Design & Branding, Equipment, etc.) but zero have images attached. Top revenue products: OMG Booth Kiosk ($1.33M), OMG Social ($301K), Print + Social ($258K). The new tier structure maps to these proven sellers.

Step 1 — Create Base Packages

Go to Settings › Products › New Product. Create one product per package tier. Upload a hero image for each. These prices are validated against actual order history.
